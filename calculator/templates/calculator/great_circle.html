Great Circle Navigation

A great circle is the shortest distance between any two points on a sphere. In aviation, great circle routes are used for long-distance flights to minimize fuel consumption and flight time.

  • Distance: Shortest path between two points on Earth's surface
  • Initial Bearing: The compass direction to follow at departure
  • Note: The bearing changes continuously along a great circle route
