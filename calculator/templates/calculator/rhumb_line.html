Rhumb Line Navigation

A rhumb line (or loxodrome) is a path of constant bearing that crosses all meridians at the same angle. While longer than a great circle route, it's easier to navigate as the compass heading remains constant.

  • Distance: Total distance along the rhumb line
  • Constant Bearing: Single compass heading for the entire route
  • Advantage: Simple navigation with constant heading
  • Disadvantage: Longer distance than great circle (except on equator or meridians)
